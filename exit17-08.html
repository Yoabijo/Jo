DAY 2,1 EXIT Questions

1. Which of the following statements do you think has the greatest validity?

Answer: C. Procurement has a broader meaning than purchasing.
Purchasing: Focuses on the transactional aspect of acquiring goods and services, including requesting quotes, placing orders, receiving deliveries, and processing payments.
Procurement: Encompasses the entire process of obtaining goods and services, from identifying needs and sourcing suppliers to negotiation, contract management, and risk mitigation. It includes purchasing but goes beyond it.

2. Which sequence is most typical for the procurement process?

Answer: C. Originator to approver to buyer to supplier.
The typical flow involves:
1. Originator: Identifies a need for a good or service.
2. Approver: Grants authorization for the purchase based on budget and compliance.
3. Buyer: Issues a purchase order to the selected supplier based on negotiations.
4. Supplier: Fulfills the order by delivering the goods or services.

3. One aim of e-procurement is increasing savings by sourcing items ______.

Answer: C. at the right price.
E-procurement streamlines processes, facilitates price comparisons, and enables efficient supplier management, leading to cost savings. While other aspects like quality and quantity are important, cost is a primary focus.

4. It is a usual practice that public procurement authorities communicate with the prospective bidders during the course of public procurement. Which of the following is the most acceptable form of communication during the pre-bid period?

Answer: A. Any bidder may, in writing, seek clarifications from the procuring entity in respect of the bidding documents.
Transparency and fairness are crucial in public procurement. Written communication ensures a documented record of clarifications and prevents any individual bidder from gaining an unfair advantage through informal conversations.

5. An agreement enforceable at law is a_____________.

Answer: D. Contract
A contract is a legally binding agreement between parties that creates enforceable obligations. It requires a meeting of the minds, consideration (exchange of value), capacity, and a lawful purpose.

6. Every promise and every set of promises, forming the consideration for each other, is an

Answer: B. Contract
A contract is a legally binding agreement formed by a meeting of the minds, consideration (exchange of value), capacity, and a lawful purpose. It involves promises that are enforceable.

7. Void agreement signifies

Answer: B. Agreement not enforceable by law
A void agreement exists, but it lacks the legal force to be upheld in court. It might have missing elements for a valid contract or violate some legal principle.

8. Which one is correct?

Answer: D. All the above.
This is a good breakdown of the steps leading to a valid contract:
- A proposal initiates the process, expressing an intention to contract.
- Acceptance by the other party signifies agreement on the terms.
- Consideration (exchange of value) binds the agreement.
- Enforceability ensures the contract can be legally enforced if necessary.

9. In a valid contract, what comes first?

Answer: D. Proposal
The contract formation process begins with a proposal, which is an offer to enter into a contract with specific terms.

10. In the Ethiopian legal system, hierarchy of laws according to their precedence is:

Answer: C. Constitutions , Proclamations, Regulations, and Directive
Ethiopia's legal system follows a hierarchy where the Constitution is supreme. Laws flow down from there, with proclamations being the next most important, followed by regulations and then directives.
