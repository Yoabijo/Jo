1. If you wish to construct a house on waterlogged ground condition, which component of a building do you need to construct to prevent the entry of moisture from the ground surface to the building superstructure?

Answer: D. The plinth level refers to the transition zone between the ground and the base of a building. Acts as a barrier to protect the building from moisture and water infiltration from the ground. A damp-proof course is a vital element in a building's construction that acts as a barrier against moisture rising from the ground.

2. Assume you plan to cast the first floor solid slab with C25 concrete for a building project. However, you notice that it's impossible to complete the slab within one day. To attain the full strength of the slab, at what location do you need to stop concreting the slab on the first day?

Answer: B. At the middle span of the slab. There are different types of joints: Construction joints occur when the concrete pouring is stopped and then resumed later. Expansion joints are designed to accommodate expansion and contraction of materials due to temperature fluctuations. Contraction joints are cuts in the concrete while it's still fresh. This gives the concrete a designated spot to crack. Isolation joints completely separate adjacent structural elements, allowing for independent movement.

3. Assume you are a site engineer for Project X, and your Foreman requested you how to construct the gutter to downpipe connections. Which drawing component will you prepare for your Foreman?

Answer: D. Detailed drawing. Construction drawing: drawing for day-to-day activities. Installation drawing: for water supply, electrical, mechanical drawing. Working drawing: most common type of drawing gives detailed information on elevation, section, specification, etc. Detailed drawing: the zoomed or the large scale.

4. What is a joist?

Answer: A. A horizontal structural element that supports the weight of a floor or ceiling. Explanation: A joist is a horizontal structural element, typically made of wood or steel, that supports the weight of a floor or ceiling.

5. What is a foundation drain?

Answer: B. A drain that carries rainwater away from the foundation of a building. Explanation: A foundation drain is a drain that is installed around the perimeter of a foundation to collect and carry away rainwater to prevent damage to the foundation.

6. What is a curtain wall?

Answer: C. A non-load-bearing exterior wall system that is hung from the building's structural frame. Explanation: A curtain wall is an exterior wall system made of lightweight materials, such as glass or metal, that hangs from the building's structural frame and is not responsible for supporting the weight of the building.

7. What is a bulkhead?

Answer: C. A wall or ceiling structure used to hide ductwork, pipes, or wires. Explanation: A bulkhead is a wall or ceiling structure that is constructed to hide ductwork, pipes, or wires and typically has removable panels for access.

8. What is a load factor?

Answer: A. A factor used to calculate the amount of weight a structural element can support. Explanation: A load factor is a factor used to calculate the amount of weight or force that a structural element, such as a column or beam, can support without failure.

9. What is a parapet?

Answer: B. A low wall or railing at the edge of a roof or balcony. Explanation: A parapet is a low wall or railing that is constructed at the edge of a roof or balcony to provide safety and/or to hide the roofline.

10. What is a drywall?

Answer: B. A type of wall finish material made of gypsum board. Explanation: Drywall is a wall finish material made of gypsum board and is commonly used to cover and finish interior walls and ceilings.
