DAY 2,2 EXIT Questions

1. What is a retaining wall?

Answer: C. A wall that is used to retain soil or other earth materials. Explanation: A retaining wall is a type of wall that is used to hold back soil, rocks or other materials, and provide support to structures.

2. What is a pitched roof?

Answer: B. A roof with a high pitch or steep slope. Explanation: A pitched roof is a roof with a high pitch or steep slope, which enables water to drain off easily.

3. What is prefabrication?

Answer: A. The process of assembling components or modules off-site before transporting to the construction site. Explanation: Prefabrication is the process of constructing building components or modules off-site before transporting them to the construction site for assembly.

4. What is a building envelope?

Answer: B. The outer skin of a building that separates the interior from the exterior. Explanation: The building envelope is the outer skin of a building that separates the interior from the exterior and includes the walls, windows, doors, roof, and foundation.

5. What is a load-bearing wall?

Answer: A. A wall that is constructed to support weight from above. Explanation: Load-bearing walls are walls that are designed and constructed to support weight from above, such as the weight of a roof or upper floors.

6. What is a lintel?

Answer: A. A horizontal structural element that supports the weight of a wall or other structure above an opening. Explanation: A lintel is a horizontal structural element, typically made of steel, concrete, or stone, that supports the weight of a wall or other structure above an opening, such as a door or window.

7. What is a vapor barrier?

Answer: B. A material used to prevent the passage of water vapor through a wall or ceiling. Explanation: A vapor barrier is a material used to prevent the passage of water vapor through a wall or ceiling, and is typically made of plastic or aluminum foil.

8. What is a beam?

Answer: A. A horizontal structural element that supports the weight of a building or other structure. Explanation: A beam is a horizontal structural element, typically made of wood, steel, or concrete, that supports the weight of a building or other structure.

9. What is a fenestration?

Answer: B. The practice of designing and placing windows, doors, and other openings in a building. Explanation: Fenestration refers to the design and placement of windows, doors, and other openings in a building to provide natural light, ventilation, and views.

10. What is a structural engineer?

Answer: C. A person responsible for ensuring that a building is structurally sound and safe for occupancy. Explanation: A structural engineer is a person who is responsible for ensuring that a building is structurally sound and safe for occupancy, and typically oversees the design and construction of the building's structural elements.
