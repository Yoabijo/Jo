DAY 18 EXIT Questions

1. If the depth of actual neutral axis in a beam is more than the depth of critical neutral axis, then the beam is called

Answer: C. Over reinforced beam
Explanation: When the actual neutral axis of a beam is deeper than the critical neutral axis, it means that the beam has more steel reinforcement than required for balanced behavior. This situation characterizes an over reinforced beam.

2. Spacing of stirrups in a rectangular beam, is

Answer: C. Increased at the ends
Explanation: Stirrups in a rectangular beam are spaced closer together at the ends where the shear force is higher, and they are spaced farther apart towards the center of the beam.

3. Which statement is not correct about reinforcement splicing?

Answer: B. The location of lapping for positive reinforcement in RC beam is at support.
Explanation: Positive reinforcement typically spans the bottom zone of a beam, where tensile stresses are higher. Therefore, it is usually not spliced at the supports, but rather closer to the mid-span where tension is highest.

4. In singly reinforced beam, the effective depth is measured from its compression edge to

Answer: B. Tensile reinforcement
Explanation: The effective depth of a singly reinforced beam is measured from its compression edge to the tensile edge

5. RCC roof slab is designed as a two way slab if

Answer: B. The ratio of longer span to shorter span is less than or equal to two
Explanation: When the longer span to shorter span ratio is less than or equal to two, the loads tend to be distributed more evenly in both directions, justifying a two-way slab design.

6. Obligatory points through which the alignment should not pass are

Answer: A. Religious structure and costly structures
Explanation: It's generally avoided to pass road alignments through religious structures and costly structures due to potential demolition or high compensation costs.

7. The extra widening is the sum of

Answer: A. Mechanical widening and psychological widening
Explanation: Extra widening on curves involves both mechanical widening, which accommodates the vehicle's physical dimensions, and psychological widening, which ensures driver comfort and safety.

8. The stopping sight distance does not depend on

Answer: C. Length of vehicle
Explanation: Stopping sight distance primarily depends on the driver's reaction time, vehicle speed, and road friction, with vehicle length having minimal influence.

9. The curve preferred for vertical curves is a

Answer: C. Parabola
Explanation: Parabolic curves are preferred for vertical curves in road design because they provide smooth transitions between grades, ensuring comfort and safety for drivers.

10. The total land area required for the construction of the roadway is called

Answer: B. Right of way
Explanation: The right of way encompasses all the land needed for the construction, operation, and maintenance of a roadway, including the carriageway, shoulders, and any other necessary areas.
