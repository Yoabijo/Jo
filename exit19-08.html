DAY 19 EXIT Questions

1. Originally Boussinesq assumed the load as a?

Answer: A. Point load
Boussinesq's theory originally assumed the load acting on the soil to be a concentrated point load acting on the surface. This simplified the analysis and allowed him to develop solutions for stress distribution in elastic half-spaces subjected to point loads.

2. Which of the following load related crack failure?

Answer: D. A & C (both alligator and fatigue cracking are load-related)
Alligator cracking: This appears as interconnected cracks resembling alligator skin on the pavement surface. It's primarily caused by fatigue due to repeated traffic loads exceeding the pavement's capacity.
While fatigue cracking typically initiates at the bottom and propagates upwards, it is still considered load-related because it's the repeated traffic loads that cause the fatigue failure.

3. The drainage layer is?

Answer: B. Sub base
Surface course (wearing course): This is the top layer of the pavement that directly interfaces with traffic loads and weather.
Sub base: This layer lies below the base course and serves several purposes, including drainage. Base: The base course provides structural support for the pavement and distributes traffic loads over a wider area.
Sub grade: This is the natural ground beneath the entire pavement structure

4. Which of the following grade of bitumen is harder?

Answer: A. 30/40
30/40 has the lowest penetration value, indicating the hardest bitumen in this case.
A higher penetration grade indicates a softer bitumen.

5. If one or more wheels act as a single load then it is called as?

Answer: A. ESWL (Equivalent Single Wheel Load)
ESWL (Equivalent Single Wheel Load): This term is used to represent the effect of a multi-axle vehicle on the pavement as if it were caused by a single wheel with a specific load. It simplifies the analysis of pavement stresses caused by vehicles with multiple wheels.

6. With the increase of width, B of strip footing, the ultimate bearing capacity of cohesive soils;

Answer: A. Increases.
When the width (B) of a strip footing increases, it distributes the load over a larger area of the soil.

7. The type of piles that are driven at an inclination to resist inclined loads and are used to resist lateral forces in case of retaining walls, abutments

Answer: D. Batter Piles
Batter Piles, on the other hand, are specifically designed to be driven at an angle (inclined) to resist inclined loads. This inclination allows them to transfer the horizontal component of the load into the soil more effectively. They are commonly used for situations like retaining walls and abutments where lateral earth pressure needs to be countered.

8. Which foundation type is recommended in highly expansive soils

Answer: D. B & C (both waffle slabs and drilled piers with suspended floor slab)
Waffle slabs and drilled piers with suspended floor slabs both offer advantages for expansive soils:
Waffle slabs: Their flexibility allows for some deflection to accommodate soil movement without significant damage.

10. Which is a primary factor that affects foundation choice?

Answer: D. A & B (both subsurface soil and groundwater conditions are crucial)
Subsurface soil: The type, strength, and behavior of the underlying soil are critical.
